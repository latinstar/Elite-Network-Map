Odebrecht S.A. (Portuguese: [odɛˈbɾɛ(t)ʃ]), officially known as Novonor, is a Brazilian [[Conglomerate (company)]], headquartered in [[Salvador, Bahia]], Bahia, Brazil, consisting of diversified businesses in the fields of engineering, construction, [[Chemicals]] and [[Petrochemical]].
The company was founded in 1944 in Salvador by [[Norberto Odebrecht]], and is active in the Americas, the Caribbean, Africa, Europe and the Middle East.
Its leading company is [[Norberto Odebrecht Construtora]] [pt].[2]
Odebrecht S.A. is a holding company for Construtora Norberto Odebrecht S.A., the biggest engineering and contracting company in Latin America, and [[Braskem]], the largest petrochemicals producer in Latin America and one of Brazil's five largest private-sector manufacturing companies.
The name Odebrecht has become shorthand for an unprecedented regional bribery scandal.[4] Between 2001 and 2016, Odebrecht paid US$788 million in bribes across Latin America.[5] Odebrecht has also  been central to the [[Operation Car Wash]] scandal.
On 19 June 2015, Brazilian authorities arrested the former CEO, [[Marcelo Odebrecht]], in connection with their ongoing probe into bribes paid to the Brazilian oil giant, [[Petrobras]].[6] On 7 March 2016, he was sentenced to 19 years and 4 months jail, for paying over US$30 million in bribes to executives of Petrobras, in exchange for contracts and influence.[7][8] That year, the U.S. Department of Justice fined the company $2.6 billion in what was the largest corruption case ever prosecuted under the US [[Foreign Corrupt Practices Act]].[9]
They eventually filed for [[Chapter 15 bankruptcy]] just two months later on August 26, 2019.[10] The group changed its name to Novonor in December 2020.[11]
In July 2022, an agreement between Novonor and [[Marcelo Odebrecht]] was signed, with the agreement, Marcelo ceases to be a shareholder in the company and ends a legal battle that started in 2020.[12]
Norberto Odebrecht Construtora Ltda., the privately owned precursor to Odebrecht S.A, was founded in 1944 by [[Norberto Odebrecht]].
