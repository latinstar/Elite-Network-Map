The [[Namibia]] Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) is a department of the [[Government of Namibia]].
It was established at [[Independence of Namibia]] in 1990, the first minister was [[Hifikepunye Pohamba]] who later became Namibia's [[President of Namibia]].[1] In 2020, Home Affairs was merged with the [[Ministry of Safety and Security (Namibia)]] and renamed Ministry of Home Affairs, Immigration, Safety and Security (MHAISS).
Beside its functions as [[Interior ministry]], MHAISS also oversees the [[Namibian Police Force]] and the prisons.[2] The current[update] minister is [[Lucia Iipumbu]].[3]
