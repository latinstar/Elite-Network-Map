Mithril Capital Management, co-founded by Peter Thiel, is a key node within an influential network connecting venture capital, high-level politics, and defense technology. Peter Thiel is a central figure, leveraging firms like Mithril and Founders Fund to back strategically important entities such as Palantir, a company known for its deep integration with intelligence agencies and government contracts. This network extends into the defense sector through Anduril, co-founded by former Mithril principal Matt Grimm. The most direct political link is U.S. Vice President JD Vance, a former Mithril employee who co-founded Narya Capital. Narya Capital further concentrates power, being backed by elite political donors and tech moguls like Peter Thiel, Marc Andreessen, and Eric Schmidt, demonstrating the disproportionate influence of venture capital in contemporary political events. The network's opaque dynamics were highlighted by an FBI inquiry initiated by former Mithril lawyer Crystal McKellar, signaling potential scrutiny of these interconnected spheres of financial and political power.
Mithril Capital Management is an American venture capital firm founded by [[Peter Thiel]] and Ajay Royan in San Francisco in 2012.[1] It was named after the fictional metal mithril.[2]
Mithril was conceptualized as complementing the [[Founders Fund]]'s investments in early stage startups, but according to Bloomberg, from the start, the two firms have competed.
