[[Elon Musk]] exerts significant political influence, notably as the largest donor in the [[2024 U.S. presidential election]], supporting [[Donald Trump]]. His political involvement deepened when he served as [[Senior Advisor to the President]] and de facto head of [[DOGE]] within the [[Trump administration]], a role he later left after a public feud with [[Donald Trump]]. [[Elon Musk]]'s ideological alignment is underscored by his support for [[global far-right figures, causes, and political parties]], and he has faced criticism for promoting controversial comments, conspiracy theories, and COVID-19 misinformation. Through [[SpaceX]], [[Elon Musk]] wields substantial power in commercial spaceflight and satellite internet, highlighted by his provision of [[Starlink]] services to [[Ukraine]] and his controversial decisions to refuse blocking [[Russian state media]] or activating services for an attack against the [[Russian navy]]. As [[CEO]] and product architect of [[Tesla]], [[Elon Musk]] commands a leading electric vehicle manufacturer, holding significant economic and industrial power. [[Elon Musk]] controls other major technology ventures, including [[X]] (formerly [[Twitter]]), which he acquired and rebranded, leading to controversy over increased hate speech and misinformation. He also established [[xAI]] to advance artificial intelligence, having previously co-founded [[OpenAI]] before his departure due to discontent with its direction. In the neurotechnology sector, [[Elon Musk]] co-founded [[Neuralink]], which interacts with regulatory bodies like the [[Food and Drug Administration]] for clinical trials, while simultaneously facing scrutiny from entities such as the [[Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine]] and a [[federal probe]] concerning alleged animal welfare violations. Early in his career, [[Elon Musk]] co-founded [[X.com]], which merged with [[Confinity]] to form [[PayPal]], where a power dynamic saw [[Peter Thiel]] eventually replace him as [[CEO]]. His father, [[Errol Musk]], provided financial support to [[Zip2]], an early software company co-founded by [[Elon Musk]]. Furthermore, [[Elon Musk]] provided the initial concept and financial capital for [[SolarCity]], which was founded by his cousins [[Lyndon Rive]] and [[Peter Rive]] and later acquired by [[Tesla]].
