[[Donald Trump]] became the 47th president of the [[United States]] and previously served as the 45th president from 2017 to 2021 as a member of the [[Republican Party]]. [[Donald Trump]] served as president of the [[Trump Organization]] from 1971, expanding his family's real estate business into skyscrapers, hotels, casinos, and golf courses. [[Donald Trump]] leveraged his fame as a billionaire, garnered from hosting '[[The Apprentice]]', to present himself as a political outsider and win the 2016 presidential election against [[Hillary Clinton]]. During his first presidency, [[Donald Trump]] appointed three [[Supreme Court]] justices, impacting the ideological balance of the judiciary. After losing the 2020 election to [[Joe Biden]], [[Donald Trump]] attempted to overturn the result, leading to the [[January 6 Capitol attack]], and was later impeached twice for abuse of power, obstruction of [[Congress]], and incitement of insurrection. [[Donald Trump]] was found liable in civil cases for sexual abuse, defamation, and business fraud, and became the first U.S. president convicted of a felony for falsifying business records in 2024. Following his 2024 presidential election victory against [[Kamala Harris]], felony indictments against [[Donald Trump]] for retention of classified documents and obstruction of the 2020 election were dismissed. In his second presidency, [[Donald Trump]] initiated mass layoffs of [[federal workers]], imposed tariffs at historical highs, and signed the [[One Big Beautiful Bill Act]]. [[Donald Trump]]'s administration has faced over 300 lawsuits challenging the legality and constitutionality of its actions, including targeting political opponents and civil society. Since 2015, [[Donald Trump]]'s leadership and political agenda, branded as [[Trumpism]], have profoundly reshaped the [[Republican Party]]'s identity and contributed to descriptions of his actions as authoritarian and causing democratic backsliding. [[Donald Trump]] considered his father, [[Fred Trump]], and pastor [[Norman Vincent Peale]] as mentors, with his father instilling the belief that he was 'a king' and 'a killer'. [[Roy Cohn]] was [[Donald Trump]]'s most important early influence after his father, serving as his fixer, lawyer, and mentor for 13 years, teaching him a transactional view of life. [[Roy Cohn]] leveraged his connections, including alleged [[Mafia]] ties controlling construction unions, to help [[Donald Trump]]'s projects and assisted him in countersuing the [[U.S. government]] over discrimination charges. [[Roy Cohn]] introduced [[political consultant]] [[Roger Stone]] to [[Donald Trump]], leading [[Donald Trump]] to enlist Stone's services to deal with the [[federal government]]. His father, [[Fred Trump]], secured a $400 million city property tax abatement for [[Donald Trump]]'s first Manhattan venture and jointly guaranteed a $70 million bank construction loan with [[Hyatt]]. After most major banks declined to lend to him following his corporate bankruptcies, [[Deutsche Bank]] continued to lend to [[Donald Trump]], though it later ceased business with him after the [[January 6 Capitol attack]]. The [[Donald J. Trump Foundation]], established by [[Donald Trump]], received millions from donors including [[Vince McMahon]], and was later ordered by the [[New York Attorney General]]'s office to pay $2 million to charities for misusing funds, partly to finance his presidential campaign. [[Donald Trump]] co-founded [[Trump University]], which faced lawsuits alleging fraud and ultimately led to [[Donald Trump]] agreeing to a $25 million settlement. [[Tony Schwartz]] ghostwrote [[Donald Trump]]'s first book, '[[The Art of the Deal]]', which made [[Donald Trump]] famous as an 'emblem of the successful tycoon'. [[Donald Trump]] adopted his political style from [[professional wrestling]], characterized by staged fights and name-calling.
