[[Oliver North]] served on the [[National Security Council]] staff, where he managed covert operations, including planning the [[U.S. invasion of Grenada]] and the 1986 bombing of [[Libya]]. As a central figure in the [[Iranâ€“Contra affair]], [[Oliver North]] orchestrated the illegal sale of weapons to the [[Khomeini regime]] of [[Iran]] and diverted proceeds to fund [[Contra rebel groups]] in [[Nicaragua]], directly circumventing the [[Boland Amendment]]. To further his objectives, [[Oliver North]] cultivated a relationship with Panamanian General [[Manuel Noriega]], proposing financial incentives from [[Project Democracy]] funds for Noriega's assistance in sabotaging the [[Sandinista leadership]]. During the scandal, [[Oliver North]] admitted to misleading [[Congress]] and shredding government documents at the suggestion of then-CIA Director [[William Casey]], and altering official records at the request of [[Robert McFarlane]], exposing a high-level cover-up network. Despite felony convictions being vacated, [[Oliver North]] leveraged his public profile to become a top direct-mail political fundraiser, notably raising over $20 million for his 1994 U.S. Senate campaign. He established the [[Freedom Alliance]], an ideological foundation focused on national defense, which has received substantial support from conservative media figure [[Sean Hannity]] and listeners of [[The Sean Hannity Show]], demonstrating his influence within conservative donor and media networks. [[Oliver North]] further solidified his influence by serving as president of the powerful [[National Rifle Association]] ([[NRA]]), a key political lobbying organization. His tenure at the [[NRA]] ended amidst a public dispute with CEO [[Wayne LaPierre]] concerning alleged financial improprieties, prompting an internal audit call from [[Oliver North]] and subsequent scrutiny by members of the [[Senate Finance Committee]], including Senators [[Ron Wyden]], [[Sheldon Whitehouse]], and [[Bob Menendez]].
