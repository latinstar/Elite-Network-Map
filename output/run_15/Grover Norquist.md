[[Grover Norquist]] is the founder and president of [[Americans for Tax Reform]] (ATR), an organization he states was created at the request of [[Ronald Reagan]]. Through [[Americans for Tax Reform]], [[Grover Norquist]] is the primary promoter of the [[Taxpayer Protection Pledge]], which has significantly bound a large portion of the [[Republican Party]] in Congress to oppose tax increases. [[Grover Norquist]]'s [[Wednesday Meeting]] at [[Americans for Tax Reform]] headquarters has become a central institution for conservative political organizing, establishing key relationships among conservative subgroups nationwide. Early in his career, [[Grover Norquist]] was executive director of both the [[National Taxpayers Union]] and the [[National College Republicans]], and served as Economist and Chief Speechwriter at the [[U.S. Chamber of Commerce]]. [[Grover Norquist]] worked with a support network for [[Oliver North]]'s efforts with the [[Nicaraguan Contras]] and other insurgencies, promoting U.S. support for groups like [[Mozambique's RENAMO]] and [[Jonas Savimbi's UNITA]] in [[Angola]], and organizing anti-Soviet forces in [[Laos]]. [[Grover Norquist]] lobbied for the [[France-Albert Rene government of Seychelles]] and attended a conference in [[South Africa]] sponsored by businesses seeking to end the anti-apartheid movement. [[Grover Norquist]] was instrumental in securing early support for [[George W. Bush]]'s presidential campaign, acting as an unofficial liaison to the conservative movement and a key figure in crafting Bush's tax cuts. Working with [[Newt Gingrich]], [[Grover Norquist]] co-authored the [[1994 Contract with America]], demonstrating his influence in shaping Republican policy agendas. [[Grover Norquist]] faces criticism, such as from [[Harry Reid]], for his perceived disproportionate influence over [[Republican Party]] members, who are described as "puppets" bound by his pledge, hindering deficit reduction. Although [[Grover Norquist]] has not been charged, [[Americans for Tax Reform]] was mentioned in Senate testimony relating to the [[Jack Abramoff Indian lobbying scandal]] as a "conduit" for funds from Abramoff's clients to finance grassroots lobbying campaigns. [[Grover Norquist]] declines to disclose his organization's contributors, but [[CBS News]] reported that a significant portion of [[Americans for Tax Reform]]'s funding appears to come from [[wealthy individuals, foundations, and corporate interests]]. [[Grover Norquist]] serves on the boards of directors of influential organizations including the [[National Rifle Association]], the [[American Conservative Union]], the [[Hispanic Leadership Fund]], the [[Indian-American Republican Caucus]], and [[ParentalRights.org]]. [[Grover Norquist]] is a member of the [[Council on Foreign Relations]] and sits on the advisory panel that nominates [[Time's Person of the Year]]. [[Grover Norquist]] joined the advisory board of [[GOProud]], a political organization for LGBT conservatives, which drew criticism from the [[Family Research Council]]. [[Grover Norquist]] signed the [[Madrid Charter]], a document drafted by the conservative Spanish political party [[Vox]], describing left-wing groups as enemies of Ibero-America linked to the [[Cuban regime]]. [[Grover Norquist]]'s stated goal is to "drown" government "in the bathtub," reflecting his extreme anti-tax and small-government ideology, and he advocates for non-interventionist foreign policy and cuts to the [[U.S. military budget]]. [[Grover Norquist]] actively participates in [[Tea Party]] politics, encouraging these groups to act as an "exoskeleton" protecting newly elected Republicans from pressures to increase government spending. [[Grover Norquist]] helped [[Arnold Schwarzenegger]] with a plan to privatize the [[CalPERS]] system, extending his influence to state-level policy.
