[[Charles Koch]] is the co-owner, chairman, and CEO of [[Koch Industries]], a vast privately held company he co-owned with his late brother [[David Koch]] and expanded from his father's original business, [[Fred C. Koch]]. As a leading elite political donor, [[Charles Koch]] heavily contributes to the [[Republican Party]] and libertarian groups, with [[Koch Industries]] making significant donations to both Republican and, to a lesser extent, [[Democratic Party]] causes. He has founded or significantly supported numerous influential ideological think tanks, including co-founding the [[Cato Institute]] with [[Edward H. Crane]] and [[Murray Rothbard]], and establishing the [[Institute for Humane Studies]] (where he has been a principal supporter and board member since 1966), and the [[Mercatus Center at George Mason University]] (where he is a board member). [[Charles Koch]] also funds a network of other think tanks and advocacy organizations such as the [[Ayn Rand Institute]], the [[Bill of Rights Institute]], the [[Heartland Institute]], the [[American Enterprise Institute]], the [[Independent Institute]], the [[American Institute for Economic Research]], the [[Pacific Legal Foundation]], the [[Competitive Enterprise Institute]], and the [[CO2 Coalition]]. These Koch-funded groups, including [[Americans for Prosperity]], actively lobby against environmental regulations and climate science, with their advocated policies influencing the [[Trump administration]]'s environmental agenda and supporting the withdrawal from the [[Paris climate agreement]]. [[Charles Koch]] further extends his influence through the [[Charles G. Koch Foundation]], which funds nearly 200 institutions of higher education, underwrites scholarships for economists like [[James Buchanan]] and [[Friedrich Hayek]], and supports public relations firms like [[Emergent Order]]. He co-founded the anti-war think tank [[Quincy Institute for Responsible Statecraft]] with [[George Soros]]' [[Open Society Foundations]], demonstrating an unusual bipartisan collaboration. [[Charles Koch]] organizes semi-annual meetings for [[Republican Party]] donors, supported the [[Tea Party movement]], and funded groups opposed to the [[Obama administration]]. Beyond politics, [[Charles Koch]] is a director for [[INTRUST Financial Corp.]], [[Invista]], and [[Georgia-Pacific LLC]], and he advocates for criminal justice reform, aligning with organizations like the [[ACLU]] and the [[MacArthur Foundation]]. He created the [[Market-Based Management Institute]] to promote his business philosophy and runs the [[Charles G. Koch Summer Fellow Program]] to mentor aspiring libertarians.
