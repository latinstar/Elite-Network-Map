[[James Woolsey]] served as [[Director of Central Intelligence]] for the [[CIA]] under [[President Bill Clinton]], though he reported a lack of direct access to Clinton, which may have limited his influence within the administration. His selection for the [[CIA]] director role by [[President Bill Clinton]] was influenced by a desire to appease [[neoconservatives]] leading up to the 1992 election. [[James Woolsey]] was a member of the [[Project for the New American Century (PNAC)]], an influential [[neoconservative]] think tank, and co-signed a 1998 letter to [[President Clinton]] advocating for the removal of [[Saddam Hussein]]. He was formerly chairman of the [[Foundation for Defense of Democracies (FDD)]], a foreign policy and national security research institute, further aligning him with [[neoconservative]] perspectives. [[James Woolsey]] served on the [[Rumsfeld Commission]], investigating ballistic missile threats for the [[U.S. Congress]] in 1998, indicating his involvement in national security policy assessment. As a senior vice president at [[Booz Allen Hamilton]] for Global Strategic Security since 2002, [[James Woolsey]] holds a significant position within a major defense contractor. [[James Woolsey]] holds a key advisory role on the Strategic Advisory Board for [[Genie Energy]] alongside influential figures like [[Dick Cheney]], [[Rupert Murdoch]], and [[Lord Jacob Rothschild]], a company known for discovering oil in Syria's Golan Heights. His involvement in venture capital, including roles as Venture Partner at [[VantagePoint Venture Partners]] (2008) and [[Lux Capital]] (2011), demonstrates his connections to finance and investment sectors. [[James Woolsey]] co-founded the [[United States Energy Security Council]] with [[Robert McFarlane]] in 2011 and is a Founding Member of the [[Set America Free Coalition]], demonstrating his strategic influence on energy policy and national security. He served as an advisor on energy and climate change issues for [[John McCain]]'s 2008 U.S. presidential election campaign, showcasing his influence in political campaigns. [[James Woolsey]] joined [[Donald Trump]]'s 2016 presidential campaign as a senior adviser, though he later resigned amid Congressional hearings on cyber attacks and Trump's criticism of the [[United States Intelligence Community]], highlighting a potential break from mainstream intelligence views. He cooperated with investigations by the [[FBI]] and [[Special Counsel Robert Mueller]] regarding a meeting involving then-Trump campaign advisor [[Michael Flynn]], where Flynn allegedly offered to help Turkish officials return [[Fethullah Gülen]] to Turkey, indicating his involvement in sensitive national security inquiries. A 2023 [[Justice Department]] indictment suggests an effort by [[Gal Luft]] and [[Patrick Ho]] to recruit "Individual-1" (identified as [[James Woolsey]]) for a position of power in Trump’s orbit. [[James Woolsey]] has been a member of the [[Washington Institute for Near East Policy (WINEP)]] Board of Advisors and an Advisor of the [[Institute for the Analysis of Global Security]], contributing to foreign policy discourse. He is a Patron of the [[Henry Jackson Society]], a British think tank, and a former chairman of the [[Freedom House]] board of trustees, extending his influence into international policy circles. [[James Woolsey]] co-authored *Shariah: The Threat To America* with [[William G. Boykin]] and [[Frank Gaffney]], published by the [[Center for Security Policy]], promoting the controversial concept of "stealth jihad" and radicalization within American Muslim organizations. He accused the [[Soviet Union]] of assassinating [[US President John F. Kennedy]] in his 2021 book *Operation Dragon*, co-authored with [[Ion Mihai Pacepa]], suggesting a particular ideological stance on historical geopolitical events. [[James Woolsey]] is a member of the [[American Committee for Peace in Chechnya]], reflecting his engagement in specific international human rights or conflict-related advocacy. His support for [[Oklahoma SQ 755]], which forbids courts from considering [[Sharia]] law, demonstrates his ideological stance on domestic legal matters. [[James Woolsey]] advocated for the execution of [[Edward Snowden]] for treason, stating that Snowden's disclosures damaged Western security, revealing his hardline stance on intelligence leaks. He has advocated for enhanced cybersecurity measures to safeguard electoral integrity, indicating his focus on protecting democratic processes from foreign influence, an issue he acknowledges involves historical interference by the [[United States]] and other nations in foreign elections. In 2021, [[James Woolsey]] was banned from entering [[Russia]] in response to [[Biden administration]] sanctions, marking him as a figure perceived as hostile by the Russian government.
