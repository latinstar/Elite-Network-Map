[[Kristin M. Davis]], known as the Manhattan Madam, operated a high-end prostitution ring in [[New York City]] that claimed to have serviced prominent clients such as [[Eliot Spitzer]], [[Alex Rodriguez]], and [[David Beckham]]. Her business extensively catered to senior management across major [[Wall Street]] firms, notably [[Goldman Sachs]] and [[Morgan Stanley]]. [[Kristin M. Davis]] also claimed her agency provided prostitutes to [[Dominique Strauss-Kahn]]. Following her prostitution conviction, [[Kristin M. Davis]] ran a protest campaign for Governor of New York in 2010 and later considered running for New York City Comptroller. For her 2010 gubernatorial campaign, controversial political operative [[Roger Stone]] served as [[Kristin M. Davis]]'s campaign manager, and she also received campaign support from political activist [[Frank Morano]] and an endorsement from [[Jeffrey Graham]], mayor of [[Watertown]]. [[Kristin M. Davis]] considered seeking the nomination of the [[Libertarian Party of New York]], but chose not to appear at their convention after the party refused to grant her and [[Sam Sloan]] access to their mailing list, leading her to create the [[Anti-Prohibition Party]]. She further demonstrated political engagement by endorsing [[Democrat Kathleen Rice]] for attorney general. [[Kristin M. Davis]] was contacted and formally testified for [[Special Counsel Robert Mueller]] in July 2018 in connection with his investigation into Russian interference in the 2016 US presidential election, largely due to her long-term employment and links with [[Roger Stone]]. During a media appearance, [[Kristin M. Davis]] identified [[Randy Credico]] as the "intermediary" between [[Roger Stone]] and [[Julian Assange]], founder of [[WikiLeaks]]. After his 2019 arrest, [[Kristin M. Davis]] became a vocal advocate for [[Roger Stone]]'s defense, appearing on numerous television shows. [[Kristin M. Davis]] also provided testimony to the [[January 6 committee]]. Her past professional experience includes serving as comptroller for [[Brookhaven Capital Management]] and later as vice-president of operations at [[Hemisphere Management]]. [[Kristin M. Davis]] was arrested for distributing controlled substances after an [[FBI]] sting operation involving an [[FBI]] cooperating witness. Her original attorney, [[Mark Heller]], claimed her arrest was politically motivated due to the [[Eliot Spitzer]] scandal, and she later engaged attorney [[Dan Hochheiser]].
