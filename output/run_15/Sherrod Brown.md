[[Sherrod Brown]], a member of the [[Democratic Party]], is widely regarded as a [[left-wing populist]] figure, advocating for a populist approach within his party and being described as one of the most [[class-conscious Democrat]]s in Washington. He identified [[Fox News]] and [[The Wall Street Journal]] as sources that foster anti-coastal sentiment among Ohioans. As Chair of the [[Senate Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs]] and the [[Banking Subcommittee on Economic Policy]], [[Sherrod Brown]] initiated an inquiry into "[[the implosion of Archegos Capital]]" following accusations of fraud and insider trading. [[Sherrod Brown]] led the [[Democratic effort to block the Central American Free Trade Agreement (CAFTA)]] and consistently opposed [[free trade with China and other countries]], co-authoring a bill to declare [[China]] a currency manipulator and supporting tariffs on Chinese imports, while also praising [[Hillary Clinton]]'s plan for trade enforcement against [[China]]. Pressure from [[Sherrod Brown]] and other [[congressional Democrats]] led the [[Biden administration]] to abandon plans for the [[Indo-Pacific Economic Framework's trade component]]. [[Sherrod Brown]] brought a lawsuit with [[John Conyers]] against [[George W. Bush]] concerning alleged constitutional violations in the [[Deficit Reduction Act of 2005]]. Campaigns against [[Sherrod Brown]] saw significant spending from outside groups, including the [[United States Chamber of Commerce]] ($2.7 million), [[60 Plus Association]] ($1.4 million), [[Karl Rove's Crossroads GPS]], and the [[Concerned Women for America Legislative Action Committee]]. [[Sherrod Brown]] has sponsored legislation to require [[corporate political action committees]] to disclose their donors and, although he pledged not to take donations from them when considering a presidential run, his Senate campaign and leadership PAC later received $1 million in corporate PAC donations, some from health insurance and pharmaceutical companies that the state of Ohio sued for illegally driving up drug prices. In the Senate, [[Sherrod Brown]] was a close ally of [[Bernie Sanders]] but endorsed and campaigned for [[Hillary Clinton]] during the 2016 Democratic presidential primary. [[Sherrod Brown]] proposed a bill during the COVID-19 pandemic to allow [[workers immediately receive paid sick days]], criticizing [[Republicans]] for blocking the proposal. He pushed [[Defense Secretary Lloyd Austin]] to establish the [[National Advanced Air Mobility Center of Excellence in Ohio]]. [[Sherrod Brown]] and [[Elizabeth Warren]] urged the [[Treasury Department]] to investigate U.S. citizens involved in the [[Panama Papers]] tax avoidance scandal. He has consistently supported [[LGBTQ]] rights, voting against the [[Defense of Marriage Act]], opposing amendments to ban same-sex marriage, receiving a 100% score from the [[Human Rights Campaign]], voting for the [[Don't Ask, Don't Tell Repeal Act of 2010]], signing a letter to [[Secretary of State Mike Pompeo]] regarding visas for same-sex partners of [[LGBTQ diplomats]], and voting for the [[Respect for Marriage Act]].
