[[Josh Hammer]] operates as a central figure within a conservative media and ideological network, serving as a syndicated columnist for [[Creators Syndicate]] and a senior editor-at-large for [[Newsweek]], platforms through which he disseminates his commentary. He hosts [[The Josh Hammer Show]], a [[Newsweek]] podcast and syndicated radio show that expanded its reach by joining [[Salem Media Group]]'s [[Salem Podcast Network]] and [[Salem News Channel]], further solidifying his media presence. His deep ideological roots are evidenced by his activism in the [[Federalist Society]] and [[Edmund Burke Society]] during law school, and his fellowships with the [[Claremont Institute]], the [[James Wilson Institute]], and the Florida-based [[Palm Beach Freedom Institute]]. As a research fellow at the pro-national conservative [[Edmund Burke Foundation]], [[Josh Hammer]] specializes in Anglo-American jurisprudence, directly influencing conservative legal thought. He co-authored "[[A Better Originalism]]" with [[Hadley Arkes]], [[Matthew Peterson]], and [[Garrett Snedeker]], a manifesto advocating for a robust jurisprudence. His legal influence extends to serving as senior counsel for both the [[Article III Project]] and the [[Internet Accountability Project]], advocating for specific legal and judicial agendas. [[Josh Hammer]] amplifies his views through contributions to major conservative networks including [[Fox News]], [[Fox Business]], [[Newsmax]], and [[One America News Network]]. The [[Southern Poverty Law Center]] criticized [[Newsweek]]'s opinion section under Hammer's tenure for becoming a "hub for opinion pieces authored by [[radical right activists]]", citing the elevation of figures like [[Jack Posobiec]] and [[Dinesh D'Souza]] and the publication of COVID-19 conspiracy theories, highlighting his role in platforms associated with controversial figures. [[Josh Hammer]]'s network further includes attending the [[New York Young Republican Club]] annual gala alongside prominent far-right figures such as [[Jack Posobiec]], [[Steve Bannon]], [[Marjorie Taylor Greene]], and [[Peter Brimelow]]. His political engagement is demonstrated by involvement with Senator [[Ted Cruz]]'s 2016 presidential campaign and a meeting with Governor [[Ron DeSantis]] in 2022, connecting him to elite political leadership. He sits on the boards of advisors for [[American Moment]] and the [[United Jewish Gun Coalition]], extending his influence into additional conservative and advocacy organizations. Significantly, [[Josh Hammer]] cofounded [[Jews Against Soros]], a coalition explicitly formed to oppose the political influence of elite donor [[George Soros]]. He has also engaged in public debates with influential figures like [[Peter Beinart]] and [[Alan Dershowitz]] on critical Jewish- and Zionist-related issues, positioning him as a key voice in these discussions. His earlier career includes roles as an editor at [[The Daily Wire]] and a contributor at [[TheBlaze]], and he served as of counsel at the [[First Liberty Institute]] and clerked for Judge [[James C. Ho]] on the [[U.S. Court of Appeals for the Fifth Circuit]].
