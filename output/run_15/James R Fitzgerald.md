[[James R. Fitzgerald]], a retired [[FBI]] agent and criminal profiler, is primarily known for his crucial involvement in the [[UNABOM investigation]], which culminated in the arrest and conviction of [[Ted Kaczynski]]. During his tenure at the [[FBI]], [[James R. Fitzgerald]] advanced to Criminal Profiler within the [[National Center for the Analysis of Violent Crime]], which later evolved into the [[FBI's Behavioral Analysis Unit]] ([[BAU]]), where he honed his skills in forensic linguistics and threat assessment. [[James R. Fitzgerald]]'s specialized expertise was instrumental in analyzing the [[Unabomber Manifesto]] to aid in suspect identification during the UNABOM case. Furthermore, [[James R. Fitzgerald]] significantly influenced the [[FBI]]'s operational capabilities by developing training programs and tools, including the [[Communicated Threat Assessment Database]] ([[CTAD]]), an extensive repository of communicated threats. Since retiring from the [[FBI]], [[James R. Fitzgerald]] has maintained his professional influence as a private consultant and technical advisor for various media productions, such as [[Criminal Minds]], [[Sleepy Hollow]], and the [[Discovery Channel]]'s miniseries [[Manhunt: Unabomber]], where he advised on the portrayal of the character played by [[Sam Worthington]].
