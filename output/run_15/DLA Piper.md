[[DLA Piper]] is a global law firm with an expansive international presence, operating through various offices and alliances across over 40 countries, including a joint venture with Kuwaiti firm [[Al Wagayan, Al Awadhi & Al Saif]] and a cooperative agreement with Brazilian firm [[Campos Mello Advogados]]. The firm significantly influences the political landscape through financial contributions, with its employees being the twelfth-largest donor to former President [[Barack Obama]]'s 2012 re-election campaign. [[DLA Piper]] employees have a strong leaning towards one political party, donating $2.19 million to federal candidates in the 2012 election cycle, with 73% directed to [[Democrats]]. Beyond direct donations, [[DLA Piper]] has a long history of political engagement, contributing $16.97 million to federal campaigns since 1990 and spending over $1 million on lobbying activities since 2002. The firm strategically integrates high-profile political figures into its operations, exemplified by former UK Prime Minister [[David Cameron]] joining [[DLA Piper]] as a consultant in June 2025. [[DLA Piper]] has engaged in high-stakes legal battles involving prominent technology figures, such as representing [[Paul Ceglia]] in his ultimately fraudulent claim for ownership of [[Facebook]] against [[Mark Zuckerberg]]. Furthermore, [[DLA Piper]] is expanding its influence into the digital economy by launching [[TOKO]], a blockchain-based tokenisation platform, in collaboration with its [[Aldersgate Digital Ledger Solutions (DLS) group]].
