[[Microsoft]] has systematically expanded its market and technological influence through an extensive [[List of mergers and acquisitions by Microsoft]], with over 225 companies acquired and stakes in 64 firms since 1986. The acquisition of [[Dynamical Systems Research]] in 1986 directly enhanced [[Microsoft]]'s [[Windows]] operating system development, underpinning its foundational software power. Through the 1987 purchase of [[Forethought]], [[Microsoft]] gained [[Microsoft PowerPoint]], cementing its dominance in office productivity tools. The 1997 acquisition of [[Hotmail.com]] for $500 million significantly extended [[Microsoft]]'s influence into webmail services, integrating it into its [[MSN]] group. Strategic software expansion continued with the 2000 acquisition of [[Visio Corporation]], bringing the [[Microsoft Visio]] diagramming application into its product line. In 2002, the purchase of [[Navision]] for $1.33 billion led to the formation of [[Microsoft Business Solutions]], later [[Microsoft Dynamics]], enhancing [[Microsoft]]'s power in enterprise resource planning software. 

A major move into digital advertising was the 2007 acquisition of [[aQuantive]] for $6.333 billion, which included subsidiaries like [[Avenue A/Razorfish]] and [[Atlas Solutions]], allowing [[Microsoft]] to exert substantial influence in the global advertising market. To bolster its search technology, [[Microsoft]] acquired [[Fast Search & Transfer]] in 2008 for $1.191 billion. The 2011 acquisition of [[Skype Technologies]] for $8.5 billion was [[Microsoft]]'s largest at the time, establishing a dominant position in VoIP services with former [[Skype]] CEO [[Tony Bates]] reporting directly to [[Microsoft]] CEO [[Steve Ballmer]], illustrating a direct consolidation of power. In 2013, [[Microsoft]] acquired [[Nokia]]'s mobile hardware division, a strategic move by [[Steve Ballmer]] to integrate the [[Windows Phone]] platform and project future influence, despite later divesting this mobile business. 

[[Microsoft]] expanded its influence into the lucrative gaming industry with the 2014 purchase of [[Mojang]] for $2.5 billion, securing the globally popular Minecraft. The 2016 acquisition of [[LinkedIn]] for $26.2 billion, under [[Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella]], solidified [[Microsoft]]'s control over a major professional networking platform, with [[LinkedIn]] CEO [[Jeff Weiner]] reporting directly to [[Nadella]]. In 2018, [[Microsoft]] acquired [[GitHub]] for $7.5 billion, significantly extending its influence over the global developer community and open-source ecosystem. Further substantial investments in gaming include the 2020 acquisition of [[ZeniMax Media]] for $7.5 billion and the monumental $75.4 billion acquisition of [[Activision Blizzard]] in 2022, profoundly expanding [[Microsoft]]'s power and market share within the global video game sector. Beyond direct acquisitions, [[Microsoft]] also holds significant financial stakes in critical communication infrastructures, including an 11.5% stake in [[Comcast]] for $1 billion, a 22.98% stake in [[Telewest]] for $2.263 billion, and a 3% stake in [[AT&T]] for $5 billion, indicating strategic influence within these sectors.
