[[Ken Howery]], an entrepreneur and diplomat, established his initial network by co-founding [[PayPal]] with figures like [[Peter Thiel]] and [[Elon Musk]], becoming part of the influential [[PayPal Mafia]]. His relationship with [[Peter Thiel]] began earlier at [[The Stanford Review]] and solidified through co-founding [[Founders Fund]] and rejoining Thiel at [[Clarium Capital Management]]. With a net worth of $1.5 billion, [[Ken Howery]]'s financial power extends through board positions at companies like [[Quantcast]] and [[ZocDoc]], and his influence on future leaders via lectures at institutions such as [[Harvard Business School]] and [[Stanford]]. His global reach is marked by his designation as a [[Young Global Leader]] by the [[World Economic Forum]] and his membership in its [[World Economic Forum Technology Pioneers Program Selection Committee]], alongside his role on the [[U.S. Council on Competitiveness]], which focuses on national economic policy. [[President Donald Trump]] appointed [[Ken Howery]] as U.S. Ambassador to [[Sweden]], a role confirmed by the [[Senate]] and later praised by [[President Donald Trump]] for strengthening U.S.-[[Sweden]] defense, security, and economic cooperation. As ambassador, [[Ken Howery]] directly engaged [[Swedish officials]] to advocate for increased military spending, U.S.-[[Swedish]] military interoperability, and "clean [[5G technology]]", which resulted in [[Sweden]] banning [[Huawei]] and [[ZTE]] from its 5G networks, aligning with [[U.S. Department of State]] policy. He further promoted U.S.-[[Sweden]] cooperation on Arctic security, influencing [[Sweden]]'s Arctic Strategy and establishing a partnership between the [[Arctic Initiative at the Harvard Kennedy School of Government]] and [[Lule√• University of Technology]]. Leveraging [[Sweden]]'s role as a protecting power, [[Ken Howery]] worked with [[Swedish officials]] on [[North Korea]] denuclearization, countering disinformation, and advancing democracy, human rights, and free trade. During the [[COVID-19]] pandemic, [[Ken Howery]] advocated for international transparency and facilitated [[Getinge]]'s ventilator production via [[U.S. Embassy Stockholm]]. His influence in [[Sweden]] spanned academic, business, and innovation sectors, engaging various institutions and addressing prominent business organizations like the [[Swedish-American Chamber of Commerce]] and the [[American Chamber of Commerce]]. Publicly, [[Ken Howery]] authored an op-ed supporting [[Sweden]]'s accession to [[NATO]] after [[Russia]]'s invasion of [[Ukraine]], articulating his stance on a significant geopolitical shift. Most recently, [[President-elect Donald Trump]] announced his intention to nominate [[Ken Howery]] as the next U.S. Ambassador to [[Denmark]], a nomination subsequently confirmed by the [[Senate]].
