[[Microsoft]] was co-founded by [[Bill Gates]] and [[Paul Allen]], quickly dominating the personal computing market with products like [[Windows]] and [[MS-DOS]], which laid a foundation for its extensive influence.
[[Microsoft]] has faced significant scrutiny over its market power, including investigations by the [[Federal Trade Commission]] for potential collusion with [[IBM]], and multiple antitrust lawsuits from the [[Department of Justice's Antitrust Division]] and the [[European Union]], leading to substantial fines and being labeled an "abusive monopoly."
Under CEO [[Satya Nadella]], [[Microsoft]] has strategically expanded its network of influence through major acquisitions such as [[LinkedIn]], [[Skype Technologies]], [[Activision Blizzard]], and the Israeli security firm [[Hexadite]], enhancing its reach across professional networking, communication, gaming, and cybersecurity.
[[Microsoft]]'s [[Azure]] cloud computing platform is a critical component of global digital infrastructure, demonstrating its strategic influence in the enterprise and development sectors.
The company has cultivated strategic partnerships with government entities, notably collaborating with the [[New York City Police Department]] for the development of the [[Domain Awareness System]], a police surveillance technology, indicating its role in state-level monitoring capabilities.
[[Microsoft]] has been criticized for its role in providing services to [[Israel]] during the [[Gaza war]], highlighting its involvement in geopolitical events and the associated ideological and political implications.
As a founding member of the [[Open Networking Foundation]], alongside major tech players like [[Google]], [[HPE Networking]], [[Yahoo!]], and [[Verizon Communications]], [[Microsoft]] influences the direction of [[Software-Defined Networking]] and cloud computing innovation.
Concerns about [[Microsoft]]'s control over user experiences and privacy surfaced with its involvement in the [[Trusted Computing Platform Alliance]], which critics viewed as a means to enforce digital rights management and potentially secure computers against their owners, and a patent application suggesting its [[Kinect]] system could monitor television viewers' behavior for interactive content.
