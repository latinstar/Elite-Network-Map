[[Kamala Harris]] ascended to national influence, culminating in her role as [[Vice President of the United States]] under [[Joe Biden]], reflecting her integration into the highest echelons of the [[Democratic Party]] power structure. Her early career was shaped by connections such as [[Willie Brown]], then [[Speaker of the California Assembly]], who appointed [[Kamala Harris]] to the [[state Unemployment Insurance Appeals Board]] and the [[California Medical Assistance Commission]]. [[Kamala Harris]] secured significant endorsements and financial support from key [[Democratic Party]] figures and organizations, including [[Dianne Feinstein]], [[Barbara Boxer]], [[Nancy Pelosi]], [[Jerry Brown]], [[Barack Obama]], and the [[California Democratic Party]] for her various political campaigns. As [[Attorney General of California]], [[Kamala Harris]] wielded significant regulatory power, leading to billions in settlements against major corporations like [[Quest Diagnostics]], [[JPMorgan Chase]], [[Corinthian Colleges]], [[Comcast]], and [[Houzz]] for consumer protection and privacy violations. [[Kamala Harris]] also engaged with major tech companies such as [[Apple]], [[Google]], and [[Facebook]] to establish policies for [[mobile app data-sharing practices]] disclosure, demonstrating her influence over technology sector standards. Her political network includes running mate [[Tim Walz]] in the 2024 presidential election and a past partnership with [[Joe Biden]] for the 2020 election, signifying key alliances. [[Kamala Harris]] held an adversarial relationship with [[Donald Trump]], receiving a minor campaign contribution from him in 2014 which she later repudiated by donating to a nonprofit supporting [[civil and human rights for Central Americans]], and later becoming a direct opponent in the 2020 and 2024 presidential elections. As a Senator, [[Kamala Harris]] directly challenged powerful figures within the [[Trump administration]], questioning appointees like [[Brett Kavanaugh]], [[Jeff Sessions]], [[Rod Rosenstein]], [[Kirstjen Nielsen]], and ultimately calling for the resignation of [[William Barr]] over his handling of the [[Mueller Report]]; her assertive questioning style even led to interruptions from Senators [[John McCain]] and [[Richard Burr]]. Her policy stances, including advocacy for stricter [[gun control laws]], the [[DREAM Act]], federal [[legalization of cannabis]], the [[Green New Deal]], and the creation of a [[Hate Crimes Unit]], align [[Kamala Harris]] with progressive ideological movements and related advocacy groups. [[Kamala Harris]] utilized her position to influence national legislation, casting tie-breaking votes for the [[American Rescue Plan Act of 2021]] and the [[Inflation Reduction Act]], reinforcing her role in shaping federal policy. Her legislative efforts also include sponsoring the [[Justice for Victims of Lynching Act]] and co-sponsoring the [[Border and Port Security Act]] with fellow senators [[Heidi Heitkamp]], [[Jon Tester]], and [[Claire McCaskill]]. Connections forged through her education at [[Howard University]] and membership in [[Alpha Kappa Alpha]] and the [[Black Law Students Association]] represent affiliation with important historically Black institutions and professional networks.
