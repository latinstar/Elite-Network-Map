[[Kissinger Associates]] was an international geopolitical consulting firm founded and run by [[Henry Kissinger]] from 1982 until his death in 2023, advising clients on government relations, strategic partners, and investment opportunities. In 1999, [[Kissinger Associates]] expanded with [[Mack McLarty]] joining, leading to the creation of [[Kissinger McLarty Associates]], which was a corporate member of the [[Council of the Americas]], an organization established by [[David Rockefeller]]. In 2008, [[Kissinger McLarty Associates]] separated, with [[McLarty Associates]] becoming an independent firm, no longer directly part of the [[Kissinger Associates]] structure. [[Kissinger Associates]] occupied a building formerly used by [[Peter Peterson]]'s [[Blackstone Group]] and was established in 1982 with loans from [[Goldman Sachs]] and other banks. [[Kissinger Associates]] does not disclose its client list, resisting a Congressional subpoena for this information and reportedly barring clients from acknowledging their relationship, thus obscuring its network of influence. Heinz]], [[ITT Corporation]], [[LM Ericsson]], [[Fiat]], and [[Volvo]].
