[[Brian Bulatao]] was appointed [[Chief Operating Officer]] of the [[Central Intelligence Agency]] (CIA) in 2017 by then-director [[Mike Pompeo]], a position involving the streamlining of operations, technology investment, and the protection of intelligence officers.
[[Brian Bulatao]] was subsequently nominated by [[President Donald Trump]] and confirmed by the [[United States Senate]] as [[Under Secretary of State for Management]], placing him in a critical administrative role within the nation's diplomatic corps.
The confirmation of [[Brian Bulatao]] as [[Under Secretary of State for Management]] was influenced by a commitment from [[Secretary of State Mike Pompeo]] to [[Senator Bob Menendez]] regarding allegations of politically motivated personnel changes at the [[State Department]], underscoring high-level political maneuvering around his appointment.
Earlier, [[Brian Bulatao]] co-founded [[Thayer Aerospace]] with his [[United States Military Academy at West Point]] classmate and future political ally [[Mike Pompeo]] and also [[Ulrich Brechbuhl]], establishing a long-standing professional and ideological relationship that preceded his governmental appointments.
Prior to his government service, [[Brian Bulatao]] gained experience in private equity as a senior adviser at [[Highlander Partners, L.P.]], linking him to financial influence and investment capital.
Post-government, [[Brian Bulatao]] became [[Chief Administrative Officer]] at [[Activision Blizzard]], where he oversees the [[Call of Duty Endowment]] and [[Corporate Social Responsibility]], connecting him to a major global corporation and its strategic social and philanthropic initiatives.
