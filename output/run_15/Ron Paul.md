[[Ron Paul]] formed the [[Foundation for Rational Economics and Education (FREE)]] in 1976, a free-market group dedicated to limited government.
[[Ron Paul]] was named the first chairman of [[Citizens for a Sound Economy (CSE)]] in 1985, a conservative PAC founded by influential donors [[Charles Koch]] and [[David Koch]].
[[Ron Paul]] is widely considered the "intellectual godfather" of the [[Tea Party movement]], which gained significant momentum following his 2008 presidential bid.
[[Ron Paul]] is a senior fellow and distinguished counselor of the [[Mises Institute]], and has actively promoted the ideas of [[Austrian School]] economists like [[Murray Rothbard]], [[Friedrich Hayek]], and [[Ludwig von Mises]] throughout his career.
[[Ron Paul]] was deeply influenced by [[Friedrich Hayek]]'s "The Road to Serfdom" and later by the works of [[Ludwig von Mises]] and [[Ayn Rand]], crediting economists [[Hans Sennholz]] and [[Murray Rothbard]] for shaping his interest in economics.
[[Ron Paul]] co-founded [[Ron Paul & Associates, Inc. (RP&A)]] in 1984 with his former chief of staff, [[Lew Rockwell]], with his wife [[Carol Paul]] serving as secretary and daughter [[Lori Pyeatt]] as treasurer, publishing newsletters like the [[Ron Paul Freedom Report]].
[[Ron Paul]] co-owned the mail-order coin dealership [[Ron Paul Coins]] with [[Burt Blumert]] for twelve years.
[[Ron Paul]] worked through his [[Foundation for Rational Economics and Education (FREE)]] to establish the [[National Endowment for Liberty]] and produce the [[At Issue public policy series]] broadcast on [[Discovery Channel]] and [[CNBC]].
For his 1996 congressional re-election, [[Ron Paul]] received assistance from prominent figures including [[Nolan Ryan]], tax activist and publisher [[Steve Forbes]], and conservative commentator [[Pat Buchanan]].
[[Ron Paul]] served as an adviser to [[Pat Buchanan]]'s 1992 presidential primary campaign.
[[Ron Paul]]'s son, [[Rand Paul]], served concurrently as a U.S. senator from Kentucky, marking a unique family presence in the U.S. Senate.
During his 1988 Libertarian presidential campaign, [[Ron Paul]] was supported by American psychologist and psychedelic advocate [[Timothy Leary]], who held a fundraiser for him.
In 2008, [[Ron Paul]] endorsed four third-party presidential candidates—[[Cynthia McKinney]], [[Bob Barr]], [[Chuck Baldwin]], and [[Ralph Nader]]—based on their pledges to balance budgets, withdraw troops, defend privacy, and investigate the [[Federal Reserve]].
[[Ron Paul]] continues to exert influence through political commentary via [[The Ron Paul Liberty Report]], a web show he co-hosts on [[YouTube]].
[[Ron Paul]] is an honorary chairman and member of the [[Republican Liberty Caucus]], a political action committee focused on electing limited-government candidates.
