The director of central intelligence (DCI) was the head of the American Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) from 1946 to 2004, acting as the principal intelligence advisor to the president of the United States and the [[United States National Security Council]], as well as the coordinator of intelligence activities among and between the various US intelligence agencies (collectively known as the [[United States Intelligence Community|Intelligence Community]] from 1981 onwards).
